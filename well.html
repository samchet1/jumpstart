Well-Being

Sleep

Getting enough sleep can enh​ance mood and promote feelings of energy and well-being. Lack of good sleep can cause irritability, stress, anxiety and bad moods. To encourage your child to sleep better:

  • encourage a routine; doing the same things in the same order before bed can help
  • make sure they're switched off from electronic devices at least an hour before bed
  • make sure monitors, screens and devices are switched off before bed, as light from devices can interfere with sleep
  • eat dinner at least 2-3 hours before bedtime

    • Routine

    A routine will help your child feel more in control and bring structure into their life. To encourage a routine for your child:

  • create more structure and opportunities for positive interactions
  • encourage down time but make sure it isn’t happening too often
  • encourage them to have a plan for eating, sleeping, socialising, exercising and relaxing

    • Self-Esteem

    Poor self-esteem can be damaging on general mood, health and well-being.

    To encourage good self-esteem:
  • encourage your child to focus on their strengths and positive thinking
  • support them to recognise the skills that they have
  • encourage them not to dwell on the things they might not be so good at
  • set small, realistic goals when doing something new, and try and reach them

    • Bone-Strengthening

    Impact exercises like jump rope, running, hopping, basketball, and gymnastics are great bone builders. These exercises help kids' bones less likely to break. These should be done at least 3 times a week as part of the daily 60 minutes.
