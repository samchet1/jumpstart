Exercise

Exercise greatly improves mental well-being and mood. It releases serotonin and endorphins which can lead to a reduction in anxiety and stress. Kids need lots of opportunities and encouragement to take part in physical activities. Seek out a variety of activities that your kids can learn to enjoy. Family-oriented activities like bike-riding, jogging, and even yard work are a great way for everyone to get and stay fit together.

Aerobic Activity

Aerobics are the most important part of a child's physical activity. Activities like brisk walking, running, swimming, biking or any other vigorous activity. Your child would ideally do 60 minutes of one of these activities each day or a minimum of 3 days a week.

Muscle-Strengthening

Kids need to build and maintain muscle just like adults. Activities like climbing, running, rowing, and even old stand-bys like push-ups, sit-ups, and pull-ups can get the job done. Muscle-strengthening helps kids feel strong and powerful. Muscle-strengthening should be included in the 60 minutes of physical activity at least 3 days a week.

Bone-Strengthening

Impact exercises like jump rope, running, hopping, basketball, and gymnastics are great bone builders. These exercises help kids' bones less likely to break. These should be done at least 3 times a week as part of the daily 60 minutes.

To help your child be physically active:

  • emphasize the importance of physical activity and its benefits
  • be a positive role model. Parents who are physically active themselves have children that are also physically active
  • encourage your child to take part in sports
  • limit time spent watching television or playing computer games
  • encourage your child to do activities they enjoy, which might not be organised or team sports
